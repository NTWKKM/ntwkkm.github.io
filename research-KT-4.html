Statistical Methods

Univariate Logistic Regression: For each predictor variable, a separate logistic regression model was fitted with the outcome variable (newrvgood or newrvbad) as the dependent variable.

Odds Ratios: For continuous variables, OR represents the increase in odds per one standard deviation increase in the predictor. For binary categorical variables, OR represents the odds of the outcome in the presence versus absence of the predictor.

95% Confidence Intervals: Calculated using the standard error from the logistic regression information matrix. CI is derived as exp(β ± 1.96×SE).

P-values: Derived from Wald Z-test (Z = β/SE; two-tailed).
