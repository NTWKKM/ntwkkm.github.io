3. CRITICAL DISCUSSION & INTERPRETATION

WHY NO INDIVIDUAL VARIABLES REACHED p < 0.05?

Despite the model having excellent overall performance (AUC 0.93), no single variable reached statistical significance (p < 0.05). This is due to three main factors:

  1. Sample Size & Power: The analysis included 82 patients with only 17 deaths. Statistical rules of thumb suggest needing 10-20 events per variable. With 10-13 variables, the model is underpowered to detect significance for individual predictors, resulting in wide Confidence Intervals.
  2. Multicollinearity: Many variables are physiologically linked.
    • Coagulation: PT, INR, and PTT are highly correlated.
    • Anemia: Hemoglobin and Hematocrit are nearly identical.
    When correlated variables are in the same model, they "steal" significance from each other, inflating Standard Errors (SE).
  3. Confounding & Mechanism:
    Malignancy Example: In univariate analysis, Malignancy had an OR of 17.16. In multivariate analysis, this dropped to 2.14.
    Interpretation: Malignancy likely increases mortality risk through other measured pathways (e.g., causing low Albumin, high Lactate, or coagulopathy). Once these are controlled for, the "direct" effect of malignancy is smaller.

  • Normal Lactate
  • Normal Coagulation profile
  • Absence of Effusion
