6. CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS

Key Management Findings:
  1. Ventilatory Status is Critical Prognostic Factor
    • Need for any ventilatory support: 94.7% of deceased vs 63.9% of survivors
    • Particularly, endotracheal intubation (36.8% of deceased vs 6.2% of survivors)
    • Reflects severity of cardiopulmonary compromise
  2. Reperfusion Therapy Shows Promise
    • 39.2% of survivors received reperfusion therapy (mainly catheter-based)
    • Only 21.1% of deceased received reperfusion
    • Trend toward benefit (p=0.162), though not statistically significant
    • May warrant consideration for acute management
  3. Anticoagulation Choice Less Important
    • No significant difference between LMWH and UFH
    • Suggests both are appropriate choices
    • Selection based on clinical factors (renal function, weight, ICU access)
  4. High Flow O2 Systems Associated with Higher Mortality
    • High flow nasal cannula and intubation more common in non-survivors
    • Marker of respiratory failure, not necessarily cause
Recommendations for PE Management:
  • ✓ Early risk stratification based on presentation and severity
  • ✓ Consider reperfusion therapy in eligible candidates (40% received in survivors)
  • ✓ Monitor respiratory status closely; need for intubation indicates worst prognosis
  • ✓ Both LMWH and UFH appropriate for anticoagulation
  • ✓ Comprehensive supportive care critical in ventilator-dependent patients
