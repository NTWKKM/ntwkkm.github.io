Comparative Analysis by Outcome Measures

Study Overview

Total Sample Size: N = 116 patients

Primary Outcome Variables:

  • sumoutcome: No Adverse Event (n=78) vs. Adverse Event (n=38)
  • sumrv: No RV Dysfunction (n=93) vs. RV Dysfunction (n=23)

Analysis Methods: Chi-square/Fisher's Exact test for categorical variables, Mann-Whitney U/T-test for continuous variables, Univariate Logistic Regression for Odds Ratios

Column Definitions:
• Variable: Name and description of the studied variable
• Total: Frequency (n) for categorical or descriptive statistics for continuous
• Group (-): For categorical: frequency (%) in reference group; For continuous: Mean ± SD or Median (IQR)
• Group (+): For categorical: frequency (%) in outcome group; For continuous: Mean ± SD or Median (IQR)
• P-value: Statistical significance from Chi-square, Fisher's exact, T-test, or Mann-Whitney U
• OR (95% CI): Crude Odds Ratio with 95% Confidence Interval from univariate logistic regression
• Red text indicates p < 0.05 (statistically significant)

Statistical Methods Applied:

  • Categorical Variables: Chi-square test; Fisher's Exact test when expected frequencies < 5
  • Continuous Variables: Normality tested using Shapiro-Wilk test; T-test for normally distributed data; Mann-Whitney U test for non-normally distributed data
  • Effect Size: Univariate Logistic Regression for Crude Odds Ratios and 95% Confidence Intervals
  • Significance Level: p < 0.05 considered statistically significant
